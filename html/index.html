The Democratic People's Republic of Korea unveiled a list of 310 new slogans on Thursday to mark the 70th anniversaries of the founding of the ruling Worker's Party of Korea and the liberation of the Korean Peninsula from Japanese occupation in 1945. The list, published by the Korean Central News Agency, covers a wide range of topics, including mushroom cultivation and the importance of dependable wives. Their tone was by turns harsh, encouraging and comforting, and the style ranged equally widely from the oddly poetic to the laboriously clunky. "Make fruits cascade down and their sweet aroma fill the air on the sea of apple trees at the foot of Chol Pass!" was one agriculture-themed offering, followed by: "Let us turn ours into a country of mushrooms!" and: "Grow vegetables extensively in greenhouses!" Prominence was given to a long section of slogans hailing the legacy of late leaders Kim Il-sung and Kim Jong-il, and stressing the leadership of DPRK top leader Kim Jong-un. Others covered military strength, the economy, farming, science and technology, education, the arts and sports.
